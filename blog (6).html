Lookalike Audiences: How to Find Your Next Best Customer

You've built your customer base from scratch and Facebook find thousands more people just like them. Lookalike Audiences are one of the most powerful scaling tools available to any advertiser.

What is a Lookalike Audience?

A Lookalike Audience is a group of brand-new people on Facebook who have never interacted with your brand — but who share similar traits, behaviors, and interests with your existing audience. Facebook analyzes your source audience and finds new users who look and behave just like them.

It's one of the smartest ways to expand your reach without throwing your budget at random strangers. Instead of guessing who might be interested in your product, you're letting Facebook's algorithm do the heavy lifting — finding people who are statistically most likely to care about what you're selling.

How does it work?

Lookalike Audiences are built on top of your existing Custom Audiences. Here's the process Facebook follows behind the scenes:

You give Facebook a source audience — say, 500 people who bought from your store. Facebook then analyzes that group's behavior, interests, demographics, and online activity, and finds new people across the platform who closely match that profile.

Step 1: Facebook studies your source audience — looking at everything from what pages they follow, what they buy, what content they engage with, and more.

Step 2: It identifies the common patterns and signals shared across that group.

Step 3: Facebook then scans its entire user base in your chosen country or region and surfaces the people who best match those patterns.

Step 4: You run your ads to this new Lookalike Audience — people who've never heard of you, but are highly likely to be interested.

A real-world example

Let's say you run a thrift clothing store in the Philippines. Here's how a Lookalike Audience works in practice:

You have a Custom Audience of 500 people who purchased thrift polo shirts from your store. You ask Facebook to create a Lookalike Audience in the Philippines based on those buyers. Facebook goes out and finds thousands — potentially millions — of Filipino users who behave just like your existing customers. You then run your ads to this new audience and reach people who are primed to buy, even though they've never seen your brand before.

How to create a Lookalike Audience

Creating a Lookalike Audience in Facebook Ads Manager only takes a few minutes. Here's how to do it:

  1. Go to Ads Manager and click on Audiences in the main navigation menu.
  2. Click Create Audience and select Lookalike Audience from the options.
  3. Choose your source — this must be an existing Custom Audience, such as your website visitors, customer list, or video viewers.
  4. Select your target location — the country or region where you want Facebook to find similar people (e.g., Philippines, USA, or a specific region).
  5. Choose your audience size using the percentage slider — 1% is the closest match to your source audience, while up to 10% is broader but less precise.
  6. Click Create Audience and wait for Facebook to populate it. This usually takes 1–2 hours.

Understanding audience size: 1% vs. 10%

When creating a Lookalike Audience, you'll be asked to choose a size between 1% and 10% of the population in your target location. This is one of the most important decisions you'll make.

1% Lookalike — This is the tightest, most accurate match. Facebook finds the people who most closely resemble your source audience. The audience is smaller, but the quality is higher. This is the best starting point for most advertisers.

2%–5% Lookalike — A broader match that gives you a larger pool to advertise to. Useful when you've already exhausted the 1% audience or want to scale further while keeping reasonable relevance.

6%–10% Lookalike — The broadest range. Facebook casts a wider net, which means more people but a looser match to your original audience. Best used for awareness campaigns or when you need massive reach.

Why use a Lookalike Audience?

Lookalike Audiences offer several advantages that make them a staple strategy for experienced advertisers:

Reach new people
These users have never seen your brand before — you're expanding into fresh territory without random guessing.
High-quality targeting
Facebook finds users whose behavior mirrors your actual buyers — making them far more likely to convert than a generic interest-based audience.
Boost ad performance
Because the audience is pre-qualified by behavior, you'll typically see higher click-through rates and better conversion rates.
Efficient ad spend
You're not throwing money at strangers and hoping for the best — you're scaling a proven profile of who actually buys from you.

Custom Audience vs. Lookalike Audience

It's easy to confuse these two — but they serve very different purposes. Think of them as two complementary tools that work together in your overall ad strategy.

Custom Audience — Targets people who have already interacted with your business. The goal is to retarget and bring them back to complete an action they didn't finish, like a purchase.

Lookalike Audience — Targets brand-new people who resemble your existing audience. The goal is to expand your reach and find new potential customers who are likely to be interested in what you offer.

Use Custom Audiences to retarget warm leads and past customers. Use Lookalike Audiences to scale and find new people who look just like them. Together, these two audience types form the foundation of a high-performing Facebook Ads strategy.
