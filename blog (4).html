CBO vs. ABO: Which Budget Strategy is Right for You?

One of the most common questions ask when setting up Facebook Ads is: should I set my budget at the campaign level or the ad set level? Understanding the difference between CBO and ABO can save you money and improve your results.

What is CBO and ABO?

In Facebook Ads Manager, CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization) and ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimization) refer to two different ways of controlling how your ad budget is spent across your campaigns.

CBO means you set one total budget at the campaign level, and Facebook's algorithm automatically decides how to distribute that budget across your ad sets based on which ones are performing best.

ABO means you manually assign a specific budget to each individual ad set, giving you full control over exactly how much each audience or creative receives.

How they compare side by side

Where budget is set
CBO: At the campaign level. ABO: At the ad set level.
Who controls spending
CBO: Facebook's algorithm decides. ABO: You decide how much each ad set gets.
Optimization
CBO: Budget shifts automatically to the best-performing ad set. ABO: Each ad set spends exactly what you assign.
Efficiency
CBO: More efficient but less predictable. ABO: More control but possibly less efficient.
Flexibility
CBO: Budget moves between ad sets freely. ABO: Budget is fixed per ad set.
Best Use Case
CBO: Testing audience variations or scaling. ABO: Equal budget testing or strict spend control.

A real-world example

Let's say you have a ₱1,000 total budget and two ad sets running at the same time.

With CBO (₱1,000 set at the campaign level): If Ad Set A is performing better, Facebook might automatically give it ₱700 while Ad Set B only receives ₱300 — without you doing anything.
With ABO (₱500 set per ad set manually): Both ad sets receive exactly ₱500 each, regardless of which one is performing better.

When should you use CBO?

CBO works best in situations where you want Facebook to do the heavy lifting for you. Here are the ideal scenarios for using CBO:

You trust Facebook's algorithm. If you've been running ads long enough for Facebook to have good data on your account, CBO can find efficiencies you'd miss manually.

You're testing multiple audiences or creatives. CBO is great when you want to put several ad sets against each other and let performance determine the winner automatically.

Your goal is maximum results for your budget. Because Facebook actively shifts spend toward what's working, CBO often squeezes more conversions out of the same budget.

When should you use ABO?

ABO gives you control — and control matters in certain situations. Use ABO when:

You want fair, even testing. If you're running an A/B test and need each ad set to receive the same amount of spend to compare results accurately, ABO ensures no set gets starved of budget.

You need strict budget control. ABO is ideal when you're managing spend across different audiences or business objectives and can't afford for Facebook to shift budget unpredictably.

You're targeting different locations, age groups, or objectives. When your ad sets serve very different purposes, you'll want to control the budget for each one independently.

Which one should you start with?

If you're just starting out, ABO is generally the safer choice. It lets you stay in control of your spend while you're still learning how your audience responds to your ads. Once you have winning ad sets and creatives with proven results, you can switch to CBO to scale more efficiently.
