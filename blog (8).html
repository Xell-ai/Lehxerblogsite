How to Properly Distribute Your Facebook Ads Budget

Spending money on ads is easy. Spending it in the right places is the skill. A proper budget distribution strategy ensures every peso you put in is working toward a real result, whether that's finding new customers, winning back warm leads, or discovering your next winning creative.

Why budget distribution matters

Most beginners make the mistake of putting all their budget into one campaign and hoping for the best. The problem with that approach is that different audiences require different strategies — and if you're not allocating your budget across those strategies intentionally, you're leaving results on the table.

A well-distributed budget works across three key areas at the same time: reaching new people, re-engaging warm audiences, and constantly testing fresh creatives to find your next winner. Each of these serves a different purpose in your overall ad strategy, and each deserves its own dedicated slice of your budget.

The three budget buckets

Here's the recommended budget distribution framework used by experienced media buyers:

Normal Ad Sets / Scaling — 70%
The largest portion of your budget goes toward your proven, performing campaigns. These are the ad sets that are already delivering results your job here is to maintain and scale them without disrupting what's working.
Retargeting — 20%
A fifth of your budget is dedicated to re-engaging warm audiences people who already visited your website, viewed your content, or added to cart but didn't purchase. These audiences convert at higher rates, so this budget often delivers your lowest cost per purchase.
Creative Testing — 10%
A small but critical portion goes toward testing new images, videos, and ad copy. This is how you discover tomorrow's winning creatives. Without this bucket, your scaling campaigns will eventually run dry of fresh, high-performing content.

Breaking down each bucket

Normal Ad Sets / Scaling — 70%

This is the engine of your ad account. The 70% scaling budget goes toward the campaigns and ad sets that have already proven themselves they're generating sales, leads, or whatever result your business needs at an acceptable cost.

The goal here is not to experiment. It's to maintain stable delivery and gradually increase spend on what's working. If you're using CBO, Facebook will automatically push more budget toward the best-performing ad sets within this campaign. If you're using ABO, you'll scale manually by increasing budgets on winning ad sets by no more than 20–30% at a time to avoid disrupting the algorithm's learning phase.

Think of the 70% bucket as your revenue generator. It's where your proven winners live, and your job is to protect and grow it

Retargeting — 20%

Your retargeting budget is focused on people who already know you warm audiences who showed real intent but didn't convert yet. These are some of the highest-value people you can advertise to, because they've already taken a step toward buying.

Common retargeting audiences include website visitors from the last 30 to 180 days, people who viewed your product pages, people who added items to cart, and people who engaged with your Facebook or Instagram content. Because these audiences are smaller and more qualified, they don't need a massive budget, but they absolutely need consistent attention.

Your retargeting ads should speak to where these people are in the buying journey. They've already seen your product — so your message should address hesitation, build urgency, or offer a compelling reason to come back and complete the purchase.

Creative Testing — 10%

Even the best-performing ad eventually gets tired. Audiences see it too many times, engagement drops, costs rise, and results fall. Creative testing is how you stay ahead of that fatigue by constantly feeding your ad account with fresh content.

The 10% creative testing budget is deliberately small because the goal isn't to drive sales right now — it's to gather data cheaply. You're running new images, new videos, new hooks, and new copy against a broad audience to see what resonates. The ads that perform well in testing get promoted into your scaling campaign. The ones that don't get cut quickly before they drain your budget.

A good rule of thumb: always have at least two to three new creatives being tested at any given time. Your scaling campaign's future depends entirely on the winners you find in testing.

Putting it all together

Let's say your total monthly Facebook Ads budget is ₱10,000. Here's what that distribution looks like in practice:

₱7,000 — Scaling
Goes to your proven, best-performing campaigns. Maintain stable delivery and gradually increase budget on winning ad sets.
₱2,000 — Retargeting
Re-engage website visitors, add-to-cart users, and content engagers. Keep messaging relevant to where they are in the buying journey.
₱1,000 — Creative Testing
Test 2–4 new creatives at a time. Promote winners to scaling. Cut losers fast to protect the budget.
