How to Set Up Your Facebook Ads Manager from Scratch

Before you can run your first ad, you need to access the right tool. Facebook Ads Manager is the control center for everything — campaigns, budgets, audiences, and performance data. Here's how to get in.

How to access Ads Manager on Desktop

Getting to Ads Manager from a browser is straightforward. Just follow these steps:

  1. Go to Facebook.com and log in to your account.
  2. In the top-right corner, click on the grid icon — also known as the menu or "All Tools" button.
  3. Select "Ads Manager" under the "Professional" or "Business" section.
Shortcut: You can skip the menu entirely and go directly to facebook.com/adsmanager from any browser.

What is the Facebook Pixel — and why do you need it?

Once you're inside Ads Manager, one of the first things you'll want to set up is the Facebook Pixel. Understanding what it is and what it does will make a huge difference in your advertising results.

The Facebook Pixel is a small piece of code that you install on your website. Once installed, it silently tracks what visitors do on your site — what pages they view, what products they click on, what they add to their cart, and whether they complete a purchase.

This data is then sent back to Facebook, which uses it to:

Track Conversions
See exactly which Facebook ads led to purchases, sign-ups, or other actions on your website.
Optimize Ads
Facebook learns who is most likely to convert and shows your ads to more people like them.
Build Audiences
Create Custom Audiences based on specific actions — like people who viewed a product but didn't buy.
Retarget Visitors
Show follow-up ads to people who already visited your store, reminding them to come back and complete their purchase.

Why the Pixel matters

With the Pixel installed, Facebook can identify patterns in your buyers their age, location, interests, and online behavior and use that information to find more people just like them. This is what makes Facebook Ads so powerful compared to traditional advertising.

Think of the Pixel as the brain behind your ads. The longer it runs and the more data it collects, the smarter your campaigns become — and the lower your cost per result.

How to check if your Pixel is working

After installing the Pixel on your website, you'll want to verify that it's firing correctly. Here's the quickest way to do it:

  1. Install the Meta Pixel Helper Chrome extension from the Chrome Web Store.
  2. Visit your website while the extension is active.
  3. Click the extension icon in your browser — if your Pixel is installed correctly, it will show a green checkmark and the Pixel ID.
  4. You can also verify inside Ads Manager by going to Events Manager and checking if your Pixel is receiving activity data.

One thing to Remember

Setting up Ads Manager and installing the Pixel might feel like boring work, but this is the foundation everything else is built on. Take the time to do it right from the start.
