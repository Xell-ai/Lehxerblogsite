The Beginner's Guide to Facebook Ads: Everything You Need to Know

With nearly 3 billion monthly active users, Facebook remains one of the most powerful advertising platforms in the world. If you've been thinking about running ads but don't know where to start, this guide is for you.

Why advertise on Facebook?

Before spending a single peso or dollar, it's worth understanding why Facebook Ads is worth your time. Here are the top reasons businesses from small online shops to large brands choose Facebook as their ad platform:

Massive reach. Facebook has over 2.93 billion monthly active users. No matter your niche, your audience is almost certainly on the platform.

Laser-precise targeting. You can target people by age, location, interests, behaviors, and even life events. Want to reach men aged 20–35 in Metro Manila who are interested in streetwear? You can do exactly that.

Remarketing power. Facebook lets you re-engage people who have already visited your website, watched your video, or added items to their cart — but didn't buy yet. These warm audiences convert at much higher rates.

Flexible ad formats. From single images to videos, carousels, and stories. Facebook gives you many creative options to suit your product and message.

Cost-effective when done right. Facebook Ads can be surprisingly affordable when set up properly. You control your daily or lifetime budget, and you can start with as little as a few dollars a day.

Goal-based campaigns. Whether you want brand awareness, website traffic, or direct sales, Facebook lets you choose the right campaign type to match your business goal.

The three-level structure of Facebook Ads

One of the first things beginners need to understand is how Facebook Ads are organized. Everything lives inside a three-level hierarchy:

Campaign → Ad Set → Ad. Think of the Campaign as the goal, the Ad Set as the audience and budget, and the Ad as the actual content your customer sees.

Campaign — This is where you define your overall marketing objective. Are you looking for more sales? More website traffic? More messages? Your campaign objective tells Facebook what result to optimize for.

Ad Set — Inside each campaign, you create one or more ad sets. This is where you define who sees your ads (your target audience), how much you spend, when the ads run, and where they appear (Facebook feed, Instagram, Stories, etc.).

Ad — This is the actual creative your audience will see — the image or video, the ad copy, the headline, and the call-to-action button like "Shop Now" or "Learn More."

Key Facebook Ads terms you must know

Facebook Ads comes with its own language. Here are the most important terms you'll encounter as a beginner:

CPM
Cost per 1,000 impressions — how much you pay for your ad to be shown 1,000 times.
CTR
Click-through rate — the percentage of people who clicked your ad after seeing it.
ROAS
Return on Ad Spend — how much revenue you earn for every dollar spent on ads.
ATC
Add to Cart — people who added your product to their cart after seeing the ad.
CBO
Campaign Budget Optimization — Facebook automatically distributes your budget across ad sets.
ABO
Ad Set Budget Optimization — you manually set the budget for each individual ad set.
Reach
The number of unique people who saw your ad at least once.
Pixel
A piece of code on your website that tracks user actions and helps optimize your ads.

What campaign objective should you choose?

When creating a campaign, Facebook asks you to choose an objective. Here's a simple breakdown of the most common ones and when to use them:

Awareness

Best for new businesses or product launches. This objective shows your ads to people who are most likely to remember your brand. Use it when you're not expecting immediate sales but want to build recognition first.

Traffic

Use this when you want to drive people to your website, app, or landing page. Facebook will show your ad to people who are most likely to click links. Note that traffic campaigns are not optimized for sales.

Engagement

Great for getting more messages, comments, page likes, or video views. If your business model involves selling through Messenger or WhatsApp, this is the objective to use.

Leads

Use this to collect contact information — names, emails, phone numbers — directly within Facebook through instant forms. Ideal for service businesses, real estate, and coaching.

Sales

The most powerful objective for e-commerce. Facebook uses the Meta Pixel to find people most likely to actually purchase from your store. This requires a properly installed Pixel on your website.

Your first campaign: a step-by-step overview

Ready to run your first ad? Here's the basic process from start to finish:

  1. Go to facebook.com/adsmanager and log in to your Facebook account.
  2. Click Create Campaign and choose your campaign objective based on your goal.
  3. Set up your Ad Set — define your target audience (age, location, interests), set your daily budget, and choose your ad placements.
  4. Create your Ad — upload your image or video, write your ad copy, add a headline, and choose a call-to-action button.
  5. Review everything and click Publish. Your ad will go through a review process before it goes live.
  6. Monitor your results in Ads Manager and make decisions based on the data — not your emotions.

One golden rule for beginners

Facebook Ads can feel overwhelming at first, but there's one principle that will save you a lot of money and frustration: "always make decisions based on data, not emotions."

Don't turn off an ad just because you personally don't like how it looks. Don't increase a budget because you feel excited. Look at your numbers, your CTR, your ROAS, your cost per purchase and let those guide every decision you make.
