Custom Audiences Explained: How to Retarget Like a Pro

Most people won't buy the first time they see your ad. Custom Audiences let you bring them back and that's where the real money is made.

What is a Custom Audience?

A Custom Audience is a targeted group of people you can create inside Facebook Ads Manager using data you already have. Instead of showing your ads to strangers, you're showing them to people who have already interacted with your business in some way — whether they visited your website, watched your video, or are already on your customer list.

This makes Custom Audiences one of the most powerful tools in Facebook Ads. Because these people already know who you are, they are far more likely to take action when they see your ad again.

Types of Custom Audiences

Facebook gives you several different sources to build your Custom Audience from. Each one targets a different type of person based on how they've previously engaged with your brand.

Website
Targets people who visited your website. Requires the Facebook Pixel to be installed. Example: retarget people who viewed your product page but didn't buy.
Customer List
Upload a list of emails or phone numbers you've collected. Facebook matches them to real accounts. Example: target people who signed up for your newsletter.
App Activity
Targets people who used your mobile app. Example: reach users who added to cart inside your app but never completed the purchase.
Engagement
Targets people who engaged with your content on Facebook or Instagram — likes, comments, shares, saves, and more.
Video Views
Targets people who watched your videos. You can filter by how much they watched — for example, only those who viewed at least 50% of your ad.
Instagram Account
Targets people who interacted with your Instagram profile — including those who DM'd you or saved your posts.

Why use a Custom Audience?

The biggest advantage of Custom Audiences is that you're not starting from zero. These people already have some familiarity with your brand, which means your ads are more relevant to them and relevance drives results.

Retarget people who didn't purchase. Someone visited your product page but left without buying. A Custom Audience lets you follow up with a targeted ad — maybe with a discount or a stronger call to action — to bring them back.

Upsell or cross-sell to existing customers. Your past buyers are your warmest audience. You can show them complementary products or exclusive offers they're likely to respond to.

Build trust with warm audiences. Repeated exposure to your brand across platforms builds familiarity and trust, making people more comfortable buying from you.

Improve your conversion rates. Because these audiences already know you, they convert at significantly higher rates than cold audiences who have never heard of your brand before.

How to create a Custom Audience

Setting up a Custom Audience in Facebook Ads Manager is straightforward. Here's the step-by-step process:

  1. Go to Facebook Ads Manager and click on Audiences in the main menu.
  2. Click Create Audience and select Custom Audience from the dropdown.
  3. Choose your data source — for example, Website, Instagram Account, or Customer List.
  4. Set your filters, such as people who visited your site in the last 30 days or watched at least 75% of a specific video.
  5. Give your audience a clear, descriptive name so you can identify it easily later, then click Create Audience.

Bonus: What is a Lookalike Audience?

Once you've built a Custom Audience, Facebook gives you the option to take it one step further with a Lookalike Audience. This is a group of brand-new people who don't know you yet — but who share similar traits, behaviors, and interests with your existing audience.

Think of it this way: your Custom Audience is your best existing customers. A Lookalike Audience is Facebook finding thousands — or even millions — of people who behave just like them. You're not guessing who to target anymore. You're scaling what already works.
