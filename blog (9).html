The Full-Funnel Facebook Ads Strategy: TOF, MOF, BOF Explained

The best Facebook advertisers don't just run one campaign, they build a system that guides people from strangers to loyal buyers.

What is a Facebook Ads funnel?

A funnel is a structured campaign system that targets people based on where they are in their buying journey. Not everyone who sees your ad is ready to buy immediately — some are hearing about you for the first time, some are considering it, and some just need one final push to check out.

Facebook Ads funnels are built around three audience temperatures: Cold (never heard of you), Warm (engaged but didn't act), and Hot (took action but didn't buy). Each stage gets its own campaign, targeting, and message.

Important: There is no guaranteed formula — you must test every setup in your own ad account. What works for one business might not work for another. Use these structures as a starting point, not a fixed rule.

The Basic Funnel

The Basic Funnel is ideal for beginners and businesses with smaller budgets. It keeps things simple with three clearly separated campaigns, each targeting a different stage of the buying journey.

Top of Funnel (TOF) — Cold Audience

These are people who have never interacted with your ads, page, or website before. Your goal at this stage is to introduce your brand and get them interested.

Inclusions: Broad Audience (no targeting, let Facebook's algorithm decide), Lookalike Audiences (1%–3%), and Interest-Based Audiences (e.g., "Ukay-ukay", "Streetwear", "Shopee").

Exclusions: Website Visitors (last 30 days), View Content (last 30 days), Add to Cart (last 30 days), and Purchasers (last 180 days).

Middle of Funnel (MOF) — Warm Audience

These are people who have already engaged with your content — they liked a post, watched a video, or visited your page — but haven't taken any strong action yet like visiting your product page or adding to cart.

Inclusions: Facebook Page Engagers (last 30 days), Instagram Engagers (last 30 days), and Website Visitors (last 30 days).

Exclusions: View Content (last 30 days), Add to Cart (last 30 days), and Purchasers (last 180 days).

Bottom of Funnel (BOF) — Hot Audience

These people have already taken strong actions — they viewed your product or added it to their cart — but haven't completed the purchase yet. This is your highest-intent audience and your best opportunity to convert.

Inclusions: View Content (last 30 days) and Add to Cart (last 30 days).

Exclusions: Purchasers (last 180 days).

Post-Purchase — Existing Customers

These are people who have already bought from you. Don't ignore them — they're your most valuable audience for upselling, cross-selling, loyalty campaigns, and asking for reviews or referrals.

Inclusions: Purchasers (last 180 days).

The High-Spend Funnel

Once you're spending more on ads and have proven creatives, the High-Spend Funnel gives you a more advanced structure built around three dedicated campaigns — Prospecting, Scaling, and Creative Testing.

1. Prospecting Campaign — Cold Audience

The goal here is to find and attract brand-new potential customers who have never interacted with your brand before. This campaign focuses entirely on cold audiences.

Inclusions: Broad Audience (no detailed targeting), Lookalike Audiences (1%–3%), and Interest-Based Audiences.

Exclusions: Website Visitors (last 30 days), View Content (last 30 days), Add to Cart (last 30 days), and Purchasers (last 180 days).

2. Scaling + Retargeting Campaign — CBO

This is the campaign where you increase budget on what's already working. It runs on Campaign Budget Optimization, letting Facebook allocate spend automatically to the best-performing ad sets. It also handles warm audience retargeting within the same campaign.

Inclusions: Broad Audience.

Exclusions: Purchasers (last 180 days).

Budget Rule: Set your budget at 50x your Target CPA (Cost Per Acquisition). Creative Rule: Run only 3–4 top-performing creatives at a time. Facebook will automatically shift spend toward the best-performing ones.

3. Creative Testing Campaign

Before any creative goes into your Prospecting or Scaling campaigns, it needs to be tested here first. The sole purpose of this campaign is to discover new winning ads.

Inclusions: Broad Audiences.

Exclusions: Purchasers (last 180 days).

Test multiple images, videos, and copy variations here. Once a creative proves itself with strong results, move it over to your Prospecting or Scaling campaign.

The Loose Funnel

The Loose Funnel is a simplified version — perfect for smaller budgets, early-stage testing, or businesses that aren't ready for the full three-campaign structure yet. It still covers the essentials: cold traffic, retargeting, and creative testing.

Top of Funnel (TOF) — Cold Audiences

Introduce your brand to people who have never heard of you before.

Inclusions: Broad Audience, Lookalike Audiences, and Interest-Based Audiences.

Exclusions: Purchasers (last 180 days).

Retargeting Campaign

Bring back people who showed interest in your brand but didn't convert. Because this is a looser funnel, the retargeting window is wider — 180 days instead of 30.

Inclusions: Website Visitors (last 180 days), View Content (last 180 days), and Add to Cart (last 180 days).

Exclusions: Purchasers (last 180 days).

Creative Testing Campaign

Just like in the High-Spend Funnel, you still need a dedicated place to test new creatives before scaling them — even on a smaller budget.

Inclusions: Broad Audience.

Exclusions: Purchasers (last 180 days).

Which funnel should you use?

Basic Funnel
Best for beginners. Separates cold, warm, and hot audiences into three clearly defined campaigns with full inclusion and exclusion controls.
High-Spend Funnel
Best for scaling businesses with proven creatives and larger budgets. Uses CBO for automatic budget optimization across prospecting, scaling, and testing campaigns.
Loose Funnel
Best for smaller budgets or early testing phases. A simplified two-to-three campaign structure that still covers the essentials without overcomplicating things.

The most important thing to remember

A funnel is not a guarantee — it's a framework. The businesses that win with Facebook Ads are the ones that test consistently, read their data honestly, and keep improving their creatives and targeting over time.

Start with the funnel structure that matches your current budget and experience level. As you grow, scale into the more advanced setups. The goal is always the same: show the right message to the right person at the right stage of their journey.
